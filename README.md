<h1 align="center">Berlin Business Landscape: A District-Level Analysis</h1>

**🎯 What is this project?**

I wanted to get a real look at the Berlin business scene as it stands today. I took over 360,000 rows of raw data from the IHK Berlin (Chamber of Commerce) and transformed it into an interactive economic map.

The goal was simple: pinpoint which districts are actually growing and see how different industries are claiming different neighbourhoods across the city.

**🛠️ The Toolkit**

**Python(Pandas):** For cleaning up messy raw data, handling unusual data, and managing missing or duplicate values.

**Tableau:** Used to architect the visual dashboard and geographic heatmaps.

**[IHK Open Data](https://github.com/IHKBerlin/IHKBerlin_Gewerbedaten/blob/1d74ea0ee57036c14e3e0c68416886a67d018ac0/README.md):** The official, raw source for every business registration in Berlin.

**💡 The Big Picture (Key Findings)**
Solo-Entrepreneurship is King: Berlin is a city of "Solopreneurs." Over 84% of all registered businesses have 3 or fewer employees.

The Startup Hubs: To nobody's surprise, Mitte and Neukölln remain the "youngest" districts with the highest density of new registrations and tech startups.

Commercial Stability: While the centre is for startups, the "Mittelstand" (established medium businesses) lives in the west. Districts like Charlottenburg-Wilmersdorf show incredible stability, with many businesses active for over 40 years.

Industry Clustering: High-tech and Software are heavily clustered in the centre (Mitte), while traditional Production and Industry remain anchored out west in Spandau.
